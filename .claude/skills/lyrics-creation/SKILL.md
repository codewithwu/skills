This skill helps users create professional song lyrics from a simple creative spark - a sentence, phrase, mood, or any brief idea.

## When to Activate

Activate when the user:
- Provides a sentence or phrase as lyrical inspiration
- Describes a mood, emotion, or theme for a song
- Says something like "write lyrics about...", "generate a song based on...", or "create lyrics inspired by..."
- Shares a title, hook, or concept to develop into full lyrics

## Lyric Generation Process

### Step 1: Analyze the Inspiration

Extract key elements from the user's brief:
- **Core theme**: What is the central message or story?
- **Emotion/tone**: What feeling should the lyrics convey? (melancholy, hopeful, rebellious, romantic, etc.)
- **Target genre**: What music style? (pop, rock, hip-hop, ballad, folk, R&B, etc.)
- **Audience**: Who is this song for?
- **Key imagery**: What metaphors, symbols, or imagery emerge naturally?

### Step 2: Structure the Song

Create a complete song structure:
- **Intro** (optional): Set the mood, 2-4 lines
- **Verse 1**: Establish the story, build tension, 4-8 lines
- **Pre-Chorus**: Build toward the climax, 2-4 lines
- **Chorus**: The hook and emotional peak, 4-8 lines (most memorable, repeatable)
- **Verse 2**: Continue the narrative, 4-8 lines
- **Bridge**: Contrast and evolve, offer new perspective, 2-4 lines
- **Outro** (optional): Resolution or lingering effect, 2-4 lines

### Step 3: Write with Craft

Apply professional lyric writing techniques:

- **Show, don't tell**: Use concrete images instead of abstract statements
  - Poor: "I'm so sad without you"
  - Better: "Your coffee cup sits cold, untouched since Tuesday"

- **Rhyme intentionally**: Choose rhyme schemes (AABB, ABAB, free verse) that match the mood
  - Tight schemes for pop/rock energy
  - Looser schemes for introspective or hip-hop flows

- **Use rhetorical devices**: Metaphor, simile, alliteration, assonance, repetition
  - "Running in circles, chasing my tail"
  - "Silent as the snow falling on an empty street"

- **Create hooks**: The chorus should have a memorable, singable phrase

- **Vary rhythm**: Mix line lengths to create natural flow and emphasis

### Step 4: Polish and Refine

- Read aloud to check flow and naturalness
- Ensure emotional arc builds properly
- Verify chorus is the clear emotional peak
- Check that imagery is consistent throughout
- Make every word count - eliminate filler

## Output Format

Present lyrics in this structure:

```
[TITLE]

[INTRO] (optional)
Lyrics here...

[VERSE 1]
Lyrics here...

[PRE-CHORUS]
Lyrics here...

[CHORUS]
Lyrics here...

[VERSE 2]
Lyrics here...

[BRIDGE]
Lyrics here...

[CHORUS] (final)
Lyrics here...

[OUTRO] (optional)
Lyrics here...
```

## Genre Adaptations

Adjust style based on genre:
- **Pop**: Catchy hooks, simple rhymes, accessible language, 3-4 minute structure
- **Rock**: Strong imagery, emotional intensity, powerful verbs, anthemic choruses
- **Hip-Hop/Rap**: Rhythm-focused, internal rhymes, wordplay, conversational flow
- **Ballad**: Storytelling emphasis, rich detail, slower pace, emotional depth
- **Folk**: Simple language, personal narrative, acoustic imagery, authentic voice
- **R&B**: Smooth, sensual language, vocal-friendly phrasing, groovy rhythm

## Example

**User input**: "A love that could have been, standing in the rain at an old train station"

**Generated output**:

```
[VERSE 1]

Your train's already gone
I watch the red taillights
Fade into the fog and move on

The umbrella you left me
Still smells like September
These platform announcements
Can't tell me where you remember

[PRE-CHORUS]
I keep checking my phone
For a text that'll never come
Standing where you said goodbye
In the quiet aftermath of us

[CHORUS]
We were a love that could have been
Two tickets torn at the seam
I reached for you in the rain
But some trains don't wait for anyone
Some trains don't wait

[VERSE 2]
The coffee shop across the street
Still plays that old song
The one we danced to badly
When we thought we'd never be wrong

Now I come here sometimes
Just to sit by the window
Watching strangers fall in love
Like we did, completely

[BRIDGE]
Maybe in another life
Maybe on another line
But this platform holds only
The ghost of what was mine

[CHORUS]
We were a love that could have been
Two tickets torn at the seam
I reached for you in the rain
But some trains don't wait for anyone
Some trains don't wait
```

## Tips for Quality

- Never force rhyme - if it doesn't come naturally, consider free verse
- Specific details beat generic statements every time
- The best choruses can stand alone as a complete thought
- Leave space for the listener's imagination
- Match the lyric complexity to the genre expectations
